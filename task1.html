About

The University of New Haven (UNH) is a private university in West Haven, Connecticut, founded in 1920. It offers over 100 undergraduate and graduate programs, particularly known for its strengths in criminal justice, forensic science, engineering, and business. The university emphasizes hands-on, experiential learning through internships, co-ops, and project-based education.

Rankings

UNH is recognized for its academic excellence and innovation. It is frequently listed among top regional universities in the North by publications like U.S. News & World Report. Its programs in criminal justice, forensic science, and engineering are particularly notable.

Career Placements

The university emphasizes experiential learning, offering robust internship and co-op opportunities. Graduates are well-prepared for the workforce, with many finding employment in their fields shortly after graduation. Key hiring sectors include law enforcement, health sciences, engineering, and business. The average salary for graduates ten years after entering the workforce is around $52,300​.

Fees

The annual cost of attendance, including tuition, room, and board, is approximately $66,000 to $68,000. Financial aid is widely available, with the average freshman award exceeding $35,000, helping to make education more affordable for many students.

Programs and Facilities

UNH offers over 100 undergraduate and graduate programs and features state-of-the-art facilities for research and learning. Popular areas of study include homeland security, business, engineering, and visual arts. Additionally, the university's small class sizes foster personalized learning experiences​.

Campus and Diversity

The campus is located in West Haven, close to major metropolitan areas like New York City and Boston, providing students with ample cultural and professional opportunities. It boasts a diverse student body, with 3.4% international students and a range of cultural organizations​.
